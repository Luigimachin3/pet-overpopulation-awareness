Understanding Pet Overpopulation: Facts, Myths, and Solutions

Pet overpopulation occurs when the number of cats and dogs being born or abandoned exceeds the number of available homes. This imbalance leaves many animals without families and places immense pressure on shelters nationwide.

In many cases, pets are surrendered because their owners can no longer provide proper care, while others are abandoned entirely. Without intervention, these animals reproduce, continuing a cycle that shelters struggle to manage.

Overpopulation is driven by irresponsible breeding, accidental litters, and commercial breeding operations. When pets reproduce unchecked, shelters become overcrowded, leading to difficult outcomes including euthanasia.

Because animals rely entirely on humans for care, many are left vulnerable when their caretakers cannot continue providing for them. Shelters receive millions of animals annually, many of whom are older, ill, or victims of neglect.

How to fight animal overpopulation

ADOPT a pet from the shelter instead of buying from breeders or pet stores. Puppy mills prioritize profit over animal welfare and significantly contribute to overpopulation.

SPAY/NEUTER all pets. Sterilization prevents unwanted litters and reduces shelter intake numbers while offering health benefits to pets.

EDUCATEyour community about the realities of pet overpopulation and the importance of adoption and responsible pet care.
